  Requesting Access to the Archives       

SCDiet.org's Archives directory contains thousands of posts relating to intestinal health through diet, from 1995 to 2001. Nevertheless, some of the discussion and debate was found too confusing for new SCDers. Please visit one of the other directories for information, or follow the directions below to request access to the Archives directory.		       

Restricted Access to Archives       

 
Saturday, February 16, 2002 - Restricted Access to Archives

Based upon requests from several SCD "Long Island" listserve members, the Archives directory is now password-protected. While many golden nuggets of wisdom related to intestinal health through diet are contained therein, many new SCDers were confused or misled by the archived discussions (which covered some non-SCD specific topics).

To request access to the Archives, one can simply email the webmasters via the Feedback link. We'll want to hear what it is you are seeking and will ask you to take the short quiz below to evaluate your knowledge of the SCD before giving you a userid and password. :-)

       

The Quiz       

 
The following questions will be used to evaluate your understanding of basic Specific Carbohydrate Diet concepts. You must get a 90% score or higher on this quiz before gaining access to the Archives. Please refer to Breaking The Vicious Cycle for answers.

  1. Which carbohydrates are specifically selected for ingestion by SCDers, and why?
  2. What do goblet cells do when a disruption in normal balance occurs?
  3. Describe the "introductory diet" - what is allowed, how long does one follow it?
  4. From the following short list of foods, which of the following foods are **allowed** on the SCD after completing the introductory diet? Processed meats, canned vegetables, ripe bananas, wheat, barley, corn, rye, oats, rice, buckwheat, fluid milk, dried milk solids, refined sugar, carrageenan, cornstarch.
  5. Describe how the preparation of homemade SCD yogurt differs from store-bought yogurt.
  6. What is it you seek in the Archives section? (Please write short paragraph.)
